<h1>Biography</h1>

Morteza is currently a senior AI researcher at NVIDIA research and a visiting researcher at Stanford. He aims to bridge the gap between theory and practice of machine learning, with a focus on generative learning. Morteza's background is in statistical learning and optimization. Prior to NVIDIA, Morteza was a research staff and postdoc at Stanford, working with Pauly(https:web.stanford.edu pauly), Donoho(https:statistics.stanford.edupeopledavid-donoho), and Vasanawala(https:profiles.stanford.edushreyas-vasanawala). He received his PhD from the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities, under the supervision of Giannakis(https:cse.umn.eduece/georgios-giannakis). As part of the program, he was a visiting scholar at UC Berkeley, RISE Lab.

<h2>Research Description</h2>

Morteza is interested in fundamental research on generative learning that has a direct product impact. Currently, he is focused on diffusion models. In the past, he worked on architecture design for deep learning, understanding inductive biases of neural networks, and solving inverse problems using learning. His PhD research contributed to large-scale data science and was recognized with the “Young Author Best Paper Award” from IEEE Signal Processing Society in 2017.
